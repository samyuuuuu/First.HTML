What are the environmental consequences of computer device production?
recourse consumption
  • Inside digital devices are precious metals (like gold, silver, lithium, and palladium) and other rare earth metals. Key materials for computers, such as copper, aluminum, gold, cobalt, and rare earth elements, are extracted through mining.
  • This process often leads to deforestation, habitat destruction, and soil erosion, disrupting local ecosystems.
  • The physical footprint of mining can alter landscapes, making them uninhabitable for wildlife and affecting local communities’ land use.

greenhouse gases
  • Material processing and assembly consume vast amounts of energy, much of which still comes from fossil fuels, leading to greenhouse gas emissions.
  • The device and its parts may be manufactured in countries that rely heavily on fossil fuels (e.g., coal, oil, natural gas) to produce electricity.
  • Transporting the devices from distant countries also consumes a great deal of fossil fuels.
