How can we minimize the impact of computer device production on the environment?
Suitable Materials

    Use recycled or sustainably sourced materials in manufacturing. This reduces the need for virgin resources and minimizes waste.

  • Bioplastics: made from renewable sources like corn or sugarcane, bioplastics can serve as alternatives to traditional plastics, breaking down more easily in the environment.
  • Plant-Based Materials: Innovations in materials derived from plant sources (e.g., mycelium, seaweed) are emerging as sustainable options for various applications.
  • Sustainable Metals: Sourcing metals like aluminum from recycling processes or responsible mining operations can reduce the environmental footprint associated with extraction.The physical footprint of mining can alter landscapes, making them uninhabitable for wildlife and affecting local communities’ land use.
Energy Efficient Manufacturing
  • Adopt energy-efficient processes and renewable energy sources in production facilities. This can significantly reduce carbon emissions.
  • Sourcing materials locally can lower carbon emissions associated with transportation, supporting local economies while minimizing environmental impact.
  • Utilizing materials recovered from old electronic devices, such as metals, plastics, and glass, reduces the need for virgin resources and minimizes waste.
What are some ways to reduce the environmental impact of computer use?
Efficiency

According to measurements from the University of Oxford, a desktop computer with a screen in use uses about 40W of electricity. This was lowered to 15W when the equipment was not in use and 2W following a shutdown thanks to power-saving features. Turning off the desktop at the wall socket would save the last 2W.

  • This indicates that although power-saving settings cut power consumption by around 50 percent, switching off at the wall reduces wasted consumption even further.

    • some tips:

  • Turn off their desktop at the end of the day to cut their annual GHG emissions by 69 kg CO2e.
  • For safety concerns, turn off laptop chargers overnight and remove them from the device.
  • Close unused programs and tabs in your browser to save energy and computing resources.
Consume less
  • Only buy electronics for your needs, not your wants.﻿﻿﻿﻿
  • Avoid purchasing extra electronics
  • Buy eco-friendly accessories like a mouse, keyboard and other necessities that have energy-efficient designs.
  • Purchasing refurbished computers and components can help extend the lifecycle of existing devices and reduce e-waste.
How can we minimize the impact of computer device disposal on the environment?
Recycling Programs
  • Implement take-back programs to encourage the recycling of old devices. This helps recover valuable materials and reduces landfill waste.

    • Differnt types of recycling programs:

  • Takeback programs Many electronics manufacturers and retailers have programs for recycling used devices. For example, HP's Recycle Program accepts computer equipment for a small fee or free HP printing supplies.
  • Retailer Recycling Programs: Large retailers frequently provide recycling services for a range of electronic products. Check with local stores for available programs.
  • Municipal E-Waste Collection: Local governments may organize e-waste collection days or provide designated drop-off centers for residents.

    • Toronto recycling program

    Electronics should never be put in the Blue Bin (recycling) or garbage. The City of Toronto collects unwanted electronics for free to ensure they are disposed of safely, recycled and kept out of landfill. Electronic items can be put out on garbage day for pickup, brought to a Drop-Off Depot or Community Environment Day or donated for reuse. For additional drop-off locations for electronics, visit the Resources Productivity & Recovery Authority web page.

Donation and Reuse
  • Encourage donating functional devices to schools, charities, or individuals in need, thereby extending their lives and reducing waste.﻿﻿﻿﻿
  • Before disposing of a device, consider repairing it or upgrading components to extend its life. This reduces the overall demand for new devices.
